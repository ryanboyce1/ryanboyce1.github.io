The League of Women Voters

Assignment: Engage users inside and outside the digital realm

We were given the challenge to improve The League's digital properties. This would in turn directly impact it's ability to fulfill it's mission of education and engagment.

The scope of this project was two weeks. To get all the research we needed to present a case study, we had to think quickly.

  • 1

    Strategy

    Before we met with The League of Women Voters leadership, we wanted to research and imagine who the users were, and who the target users could be. Empathy maps and proto-personas helped us put ourselves in the shoes of the target audience. Comperitive analysis was very helpful as well, in that we found political groups around the country that were very successful.

  • 2

    Stakeholder Meetings

    We scheduled time to meet with the leaders of TLWV to talk about possible pain points in membership. We learned about their current users and found they were looking to incorporate a new generation of younger women to carry the torch and having a good digital presence is a great way to achieve this.

  • 3

    Research and Analysis

    The bulk of our project included research. We conducted several interviews and surveys to uncover what experience people of all ages have in regards to politcal involvement. This was an A-ha moment for me because I found through my phone interviews that our younger generation wasn't getting a lot of education in the political process in school. This was an opportunity to communicate that TLWV can take action in helping educate our youth.

  • 4

    Non-Digital Engagement

    From what we gathered, TLWV has a tough time engaging younger voters in general let alone the few that are using their web platform. We encouraged them to reach out in several different ways that have helped other comparitive organizations gain traction in their cause

  • 5

    User flows and prototyping

    We gathered our research and plugged it into user personas and user flows for an improved digital product. We created rough prototypes and tested them out to find hiccups in the process and then made iterations. From there we made design decisions that would keep users engaged in political process and knowledge. Our goal was a website and a mobile website that was easy to click through for quick information while keeping an archive for larger chunks of information such as the voter registration guide

  • 6

    Visual Design and Production

    From our testing and interations, we were finally able to start producing initial visual designs for TLWV website. We found the their current content needed upgrades in many visual aspects as well as it's information architecture. We organized the user flows for uninterrupted processes and easy to find, and easy to read.

  • 7

    Evaluations and Iterations

    We learned a lot from our user tests and made several iterations to our design. This was mostly included changing the wording of menus and confusing semantics, but we found the design was attractive and easy to understand. A bit more research here led us to changes to such things that kept the user confident that they knew where they were on the site, and where they were going.

  • 8

    Our Recommended Design

    We finalized our design decisions to be a sleek and attractive mobile website. We incorporated a calender to encourage users to participate and to see quickly if there were any events, we gave them the ability within a few clicks to find out who their representatives were and how to contact them, and a downloadable Voter Registration Guide so they are aware of how they can vote in local elections.

Moving Forward

When given the project to improve the digital properties of such a storied organization as The League of Women Voters, we believed it to be a very straight forward project. We were quite wrong. It's one thing to take a website and see it needs to be updated and how. We could have taken their current site to people for heuristic evaluation, made changes in colors, menu's and work flows, but we ended up really invested in this project for who they were as people.

After understanding the problem wasn't so much in interaction design, but in research of their users and how to get them involved, we stopped in our tracks and reevaluated our UX process. As a project I hoped to get more interface work done in the world of HCI, but I really gained empathy for what it's like to holistically help try and solve an entire problem, not just a digital one.
